Tesla — sum-of-parts valuation model v2
Three-scenario enterprise value analysis · Target year 2028
TSLA SOTP framework 2028E
This model applies a sum-of-parts (SOTP) valuation framework to Tesla, treating the company as three structurally distinct businesses — each with its own economics, growth trajectory, and appropriate valuation multiple. The three segments are valued independently and aggregated to a consolidated enterprise value. An equity bridge then adjusts for Tesla's net cash position and divides by diluted share count to produce an implied share price per scenario.
The model is explicitly forward-looking, targeting 2028 operating conditions rather than current reported financials — a deliberate design choice to capture Tesla's optionality in autonomous driving and software monetisation, which are not yet meaningfully reflected in today's income statement. Three scenarios (bear, base, bull) each carry independent assumptions for the six key value drivers; they are no longer simple multipliers on a single output, but distinct narratives about how Tesla's competitive position evolves.
How to read this dashboard: start with the segment overview to understand what each business unit contributes, then compare how the composition shifts across scenarios — the most important analytical insight is not the absolute valuation but the changing mix between EV (mature), FSD (scaling), and Robotaxi (speculative).
The three business segments
FSD software
Full Self-Driving as a recurring subscription platform. Revenue = fleet × penetration rate × annual fee. Valued as a high-margin software business on a P/E multiple reflecting its SaaS characteristics (75% gross margin, high incremental value).
EV car company
The legacy hardware business — vehicle manufacturing and delivery. Valued on a straightforward P/E basis applied to normalised EV earnings. This is the model's anchor segment and least speculative input.
Robotaxi network
An Uber-like autonomous ride-hail platform. Tesla deploys a vehicle fleet, earns a 25% platform commission on gross fares, and the resulting operating profit is capitalised at a premium multiple reflecting the platform network effect and capital-light scaling.

The critical analytical observation is how the composition shifts across scenarios, not merely the absolute size. In the bear case, the EV segment dominates at 70% of enterprise value — Tesla trades largely as an automobile manufacturer. In the bull case, Robotaxi alone accounts for 41% of total value ($393.8B of $965.3B), surpassing the EV segment — at that point the market is pricing Tesla as a transportation-as-a-service platform, not a carmaker. This compositional shift is the model's most important insight.
